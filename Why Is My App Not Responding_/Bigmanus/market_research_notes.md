### Major Players & Valuations
- **Duolingo**: $8 billion valuation (May 2024, $200M funding round)
- **BYJU'S (India)**: $22 billion valuation (largest e-learning unicorn)
- **Yuanfudao (China)**: $17 billion valuation
- **Quizlet**: Active AI integration, mid-market incumbent
- **Chegg**: Established player in student services
- **Coursera**: AI-driven course platform

### Market Trends
- **AI Integration**: Nearly $500M raised by AI-driven edtech companies
- **Fast-moving incumbents**: Duolingo, Quizlet, Khan Academy leading GPT wave

- **Mobile-first**: 58% of learners prefer adaptive learning experiences
- **Subscription models**: Dominant revenue strategy

## Market Gaps & Opportunities

### What Students Need
1. **Personalized AI tutoring** (not just content delivery)
2. **Offline mobile access** (study anywhere)
3. **Multi-format support** (audio, video, PDF, live lectures)
4. **Spaced repetition** (proven memory retention)
5. **Privacy-first** (no data breaches)

### Lecture Me's Competitive Advantages
1. **All-in-one platform** (vs. single-purpose apps)
2. **AI transcription + study guides + flashcards + Q&A** (integrated workflow)
3. **Spaced repetition built-in** (Quizlet lacks this)
4. **College-focused** (not K-12 or language learning)
5. **Modern tech stack** (faster, more scalable)
6. **Purple branding** (perfect for K-State partnership)
